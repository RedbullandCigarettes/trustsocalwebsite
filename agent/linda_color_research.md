- Teal: Trust, healing, stability, professionalism
- Combined: Balance of warmth and reliability - perfect for behavioral health!

## 2. RECONCILING LOGO WITH RESEARCH

**The Issue**: My research warned against bright orange, but your logo's orange is actually:
- **Softer/coral-toned** (not aggressive neon orange)
- **Contextual** (represents sunrise = hope, renewal)
- **Balanced** with calming teal

**The Solution**: Use the logo colors strategically, not equally.

## 3. REVISED COLOR RECOMMENDATIONS

**Primary Color: Teal Blue** 
- Hex: #2E8B8B (from logo)
- Reason: Establishes trust and calm as the dominant experience
- Usage: Headers, main navigation, primary buttons

**Accent Color 1: Muted Coral**
- Hex: #E67E40 (logo orange, used sparingly)
- Reason: Honors brand while maintaining warmth without aggression
- Usage: ONLY for hope/progress elements (see specific usage below)

**Accent Color 2: Soft Sage**
- Hex: #A8C4A2
- Reason: Bridges the gap between teal and coral naturally
- Usage: Supporting elements, backgrounds, secondary buttons

**Supporting Neutrals:**
- Warm White: #FDFCFA
- Soft Gray: #F5F5F5
- Deep Gray: #444444

## 4. HOW TO USE LOGO COLORS EFFECTIVELY

**Logo Orange - Strategic Placement ONLY:**
- ✅ Success messages ("Appointment confirmed!")
- ✅ Progress indicators 
- ✅ Call-to-action for positive steps ("Start Your Journey")
- ✅ Testimonial highlights
- ❌ Never for warnings, forms, or large background areas

**Making Orange Softer:**
- Use at 70% opacity over white backgrounds
- Pair immediately with teal text/elements
- Use in small doses (maximum 10% of any page)
- Always surround with calming colors

**Balance Formula:**
- 60% Teal (calming dominance)
- 30% Neutrals (professional space)
- 10% Coral (hopeful accents)

## 5. BRAND-ALIGNED SOLUTION

**Website Header**: Logo appears normally, but immediately below, teal dominates
**Navigation**: Teal primary, coral only for active/hover states
**Content Areas**: Teal headings, coral for positive action buttons only
**Footer**: Return to full logo colors (brand reinforcement)

**Key Strategy**: Let the logo be the "brand moment" where both colors shine equally, but in the website experience, teal leads while coral provides strategic warmth.

This approach:
- ✅ Honors Trust SoCal brand identity
- ✅ Applies behavioral health color psychology
- ✅ Creates calm, professional experience
- ✅ Uses orange positively (hope) not negatively (urgency)
