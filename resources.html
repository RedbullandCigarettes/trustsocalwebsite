Frequently Asked Questions

The stages of change model includes:

  • Precontemplation: Not ready to change, may not recognize the problem
  • Contemplation: Considering change, weighing pros and cons
  • Preparation: Getting ready to change, making plans
  • Action: Actively making changes, implementing new behaviors
  • Maintenance: Sustaining long-term recovery, preventing relapse

Understanding these stages helps individuals and families navigate the recovery process more effectively and set realistic expectations.

Families can support recovery through:

  • Learning about addiction as a disease
  • Setting healthy boundaries
  • Attending family therapy sessions
  • Joining support groups like Al-Anon or Nar-Anon
  • Practicing self-care and focusing on their own healing
  • Avoiding enabling behaviors
  • Celebrating milestones and progress

Professional guidance from addiction specialists can help families develop effective support strategies tailored to their specific situation.

Orange County offers numerous addiction resources including:

  • Licensed treatment centers like Trust SoCal
  • Support groups (AA, NA, SMART Recovery)
  • Mental health services
  • Community programs and outreach
  • Sober living facilities
  • Intensive outpatient programs
  • Family support services

Trust SoCal provides comprehensive addiction treatment services and can help connect individuals with appropriate local resources based on their specific needs.
