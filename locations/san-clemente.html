Serving the San Clemente Community

San Clemente, with a population of approximately 65,000 residents, is Orange County's southernmost beach city, bordering Camp Pendleton and San Diego County. The city faces unique addiction challenges: the beach bar culture can normalize heavy drinking, military families deal with deployment stress and PTSD, and the small-town atmosphere can make seeking help feel risky. Alcohol addiction is common, while opioid dependency affects both veterans and civilians managing pain or trauma.

About San Clemente, California

San Clemente (population 64,989) is Orange County's southernmost city, bordering Camp Pendleton and San Diego County. Known for its surfing culture, Spanish architecture, and beach lifestyle, it attracts those seeking a laid-back coastal life.

The city has strong military ties due to its proximity to Camp Pendleton. Many military families and veterans call San Clemente home, bringing unique challenges related to PTSD, deployment stress, and transition to civilian life.

Trust SoCal understands both beach community culture and military family needs. We provide confidential, effective treatment with sensitivity to the unique challenges facing San Clemente residents.

Frequently Asked Questions: San Clemente Rehab

How far is Trust SoCal from San Clemente?

Trust SoCal is approximately 30-40 minutes from San Clemente via I-5 North. Our Orange County facility is accessible from all San Clemente neighborhoods including the Pier area, T-Street, and Las Palmas. We provide complimentary transportation.

Do you offer transportation from San Clemente?

Yes, we provide complimentary transportation from anywhere in San Clemente. We can arrange pickup from your home, near the Pier, or any convenient location. Transportation is also available for family therapy sessions.

What insurance plans do you accept for San Clemente residents?

We accept most major PPO insurance plans including Anthem Blue Cross, Aetna, Cigna, United Healthcare, Blue Shield of California, and TRICARE for military families. Call for free, confidential insurance verification.

Are there local AA or NA meetings near San Clemente?

Yes, San Clemente has an active recovery community with multiple AA and NA meetings weekly. There are meetings at local churches and community centers throughout the South County area. We help connect patients with local support groups during aftercare planning.

Do you accept TRICARE for military families from Camp Pendleton?

Yes, we accept TRICARE and serve military families from San Clemente and Camp Pendleton. Our staff understands military culture and the unique challenges facing service members and their families, including PTSD and deployment-related stress. Learn more about our team.
