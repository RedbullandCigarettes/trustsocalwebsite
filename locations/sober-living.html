Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between sober living and residential treatment?

Sober living homes provide transitional housing for individuals who have completed residential treatment or detox. Unlike residential treatment, sober living focuses on independent living skills while maintaining a structured, substance-free environment. Residents typically work or attend school while living in the home.

How long can I stay in a sober living home?

Length of stay varies based on individual needs, but most residents stay 3-12 months. We work with each resident to develop a personalized timeline that supports their recovery goals and transition to independent living.

What are the house rules and expectations?

All residents must maintain sobriety, attend house meetings, complete assigned chores, follow curfew guidelines, and participate in drug/alcohol testing. Specific rules vary by location but all focus on creating a safe, supportive environment for recovery.
