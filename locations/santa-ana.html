Serving the Santa Ana Community

Santa Ana faces significant substance abuse challenges as Orange County's most populous city. With a population of over 310,000, the city sees disproportionate impacts from the opioid epidemic. Orange County recorded more than 700 overdose deaths in 2023, with fentanyl involved in over 75% of cases. Santa Ana residents, particularly in underserved communities, often lack access to quality addiction treatment despite having some of the highest need in the county.

Trust SoCal is committed to serving Santa Ana families with culturally competent care. Our bilingual staff understands the unique challenges facing this diverse community, from language barriers to economic pressures that can complicate recovery. Whether you're struggling with alcohol addiction, opioid dependency, or other substance use disorders, we provide accessible, compassionate treatment.

Getting to Our Facility

From Downtown Santa Ana: Our treatment center is conveniently located just minutes from the Santa Ana Civic Center. Take the I-5 South to the 55 Freeway, then follow signs to our facility. We're easily accessible from the Orange County Transportation Authority (OCTA) bus routes, and we offer transportation assistance for those who need it.

Need a ride? Call us at 555-123-4567 and we'll arrange transportation from anywhere in Santa Ana.

Frequently Asked Questions: Santa Ana Drug Rehab

How far is Trust SoCal from downtown Santa Ana?

Our treatment facility is located just 10-15 minutes from downtown Santa Ana via the 55 Freeway. We're easily accessible from all Santa Ana neighborhoods, and we offer complimentary transportation for patients who need it.

Do you offer transportation from Santa Ana?

Yes, we provide free transportation assistance for Santa Ana residents entering treatment. Our admissions team can arrange pickup from your home, a hospital, or any location in Santa Ana. Just call 555-123-4567 to coordinate.

What insurance plans do you accept for Santa Ana residents?

We accept most major insurance plans including Aetna, Blue Cross Blue Shield, Cigna, Humana, Kaiser, United Healthcare, and many others. Many Santa Ana residents have coverage through Medi-Cal, which we also accept. Visit our insurance page or call for free verification.

Are there local AA/NA meetings near Santa Ana?

Yes, Santa Ana has numerous AA and NA meetings, including Spanish-language options. The Santa Ana Alano Club hosts daily meetings, and the Orange County AA Central Office at (714) 556-4555 can help you find meetings near you. We also help connect patients with local support groups as part of our aftercare planning.

Do you have Spanish-speaking staff for Santa Ana patients?

Absolutely. We understand that Santa Ana is a diverse, predominantly Hispanic community. Our facility has bilingual Spanish-English staff available to provide culturally sensitive care. All intake, therapy, and support services can be conducted in Spanish upon request.
