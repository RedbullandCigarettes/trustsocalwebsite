Addiction Treatment for the Fullerton Community

Fullerton is home to Cal State Fullerton, Fullerton College, and a vibrant downtown scene—but the combination of college culture, bar-heavy nightlife, and academic pressure creates an environment where substance abuse can escalate quickly.

Substance Abuse in College Communities

  • Binge drinking is normalized in college social settings
  • Study drugs (Adderall, Vyvanse) are widely misused for academic performance
  • Greek life and party culture can accelerate addiction
  • Depression and anxiety often co-occur with substance use

Why Young Adults Choose Trust SoCal

  • Academic coordination with university medical withdrawal if needed
  • Life skills training for independent living after treatment
  • Career/education planning to get back on track

Frequently Asked Questions: Fullerton Rehab

Can I take a medical withdrawal from CSUF for treatment?

Yes. Many students take medical withdrawals to address addiction. We can provide documentation for your university, and treatment may help protect your academic record.

Will my parents find out?

If you're over 18, your treatment is confidential under HIPAA. We don't contact anyone without your written consent. Many young adults choose to involve family, but it's your choice.

Do you treat Adderall and study drug addiction?

Yes. Stimulant addiction is common among college students. We provide medical detox if needed and help you develop healthy study habits and coping strategies.

Does insurance cover rehab for college students?

Many students are covered under parent's insurance until age 26. We verify insurance confidentially and can explain your coverage options.
