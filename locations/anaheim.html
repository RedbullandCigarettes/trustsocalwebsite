Serving the Anaheim Community

Anaheim is Orange County's largest city by area and faces unique addiction challenges. With a population of over 350,000, Anaheim is a diverse community that includes everything from tourist areas around Disneyland to working-class neighborhoods in central and west Anaheim. Orange County experienced over 700 overdose deaths in 2023, with Anaheim representing a significant portion of those tragedies. The fentanyl crisis has particularly impacted younger residents.

Trust SoCal is proud to serve Anaheim families with accessible, evidence-based treatment. Whether you're struggling with alcohol addiction, opioid dependency, or other substance use challenges, we provide comprehensive care including medical detox, residential treatment, and flexible outpatient programs.

Frequently Asked Questions: Anaheim Drug Rehab

How far is Trust SoCal from Anaheim?

Our treatment facility is located approximately 15-20 minutes from Anaheim via the 57 and 55 freeways. We're easily accessible from all Anaheim neighborhoods including Anaheim Hills, the Resort District, and Platinum Triangle area.

Do you offer transportation from Anaheim?

Yes, we provide complimentary transportation for Anaheim residents entering treatment. We can pick you up from home, work, the Anaheim Metrolink station, or any location. Call (555) 123-4567 to arrange pickup.

What insurance plans do you accept for Anaheim residents?

We accept most major insurance plans including Aetna, Blue Cross Blue Shield, Cigna, Kaiser Permanente, United Healthcare, and Medi-Cal. Many Anaheim residents have coverage through Disneyland Resort, Honda Center employers, or other local companies. Visit our insurance page for free verification.

Are there AA/NA meetings in Anaheim?

Yes, Anaheim has numerous recovery meetings including the Anaheim Alano Club at 202 W Broadway which hosts daily AA meetings. The OC AA Central Office at (714) 556-4555 can help you find meetings throughout Anaheim and Orange County. We help connect patients with local support groups as part of aftercare.

Do you have Spanish-speaking counselors for Anaheim patients?

Yes, we have bilingual Spanish-English staff available. Anaheim is a diverse community, and we're committed to providing culturally competent care. All intake, therapy, and support services can be provided in Spanish upon request.
