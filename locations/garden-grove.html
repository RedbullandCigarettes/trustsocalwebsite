Serving the Garden Grove Community

Garden Grove (population approximately 172,000) is the fifth-largest city in Orange County and home to one of the largest Vietnamese-American communities in the nation. The city's diverse population includes significant Vietnamese, Korean, and Hispanic communities. Like all of Orange County, Garden Grove is affected by the regional addiction crisis, with the county reporting over 500 overdose deaths annually. Cultural stigma around addiction can make it especially difficult for residents to seek help.

Frequently Asked Questions: Garden Grove Rehab

How far is Trust SoCal from Garden Grove?

Trust SoCal is located approximately 10-15 minutes from Garden Grove. We are easily accessible via the 22 Freeway (Garden Grove Freeway) and serve the entire Garden Grove community including Little Saigon.

Do you offer transportation from Garden Grove?

Yes, we provide complimentary transportation coordination for Garden Grove residents. We can arrange pickup from your home, the Little Saigon area, or anywhere in Garden Grove.

What insurance plans do you accept for Garden Grove residents?

We accept most major insurance plans including Anthem Blue Cross, Aetna, Cigna, United Healthcare, Medi-Cal, and many others. Verify your insurance for free.

Do you have Vietnamese or Korean speaking staff?

Yes, given Garden Grove's diverse community, we have multilingual staff and can provide culturally sensitive treatment. Our clinical team understands the unique cultural considerations of the Asian-American community.

Are there AA or NA meetings near Garden Grove?

Yes, there are numerous AA and NA meetings in Garden Grove and surrounding areas. The Orange County Central Office (714-556-4555) can help locate meetings, including Vietnamese-speaking meetings.
