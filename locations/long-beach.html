Serving the Long Beach Community

Long Beach (population approximately 466,000) is California's seventh-largest city and home to one of the world's busiest ports. The city faces significant addiction challenges, with Los Angeles County reporting over 2,400 overdose deaths annually. Long Beach's diverse population includes port workers, CSULB students, aerospace employees, and a vibrant LGBTQ+ community in the East Village. Trust SoCal provides culturally competent, inclusive treatment for all Long Beach residents.

Frequently Asked Questions: Long Beach Drug Rehab

How far is Trust SoCal from Long Beach?

Trust SoCal is located approximately 20-30 minutes from Long Beach via the 405 or 22 freeways. We serve all Long Beach neighborhoods including Downtown, Belmont Shore, Signal Hill, and the port area.

Do you offer transportation from Long Beach?

Yes, we provide complimentary transportation coordination for Long Beach residents. We can arrange pickup from anywhere in Long Beach, including Downtown, the CSULB area, or surrounding communities like Signal Hill and Lakewood.

What insurance plans do you accept for Long Beach residents?

We accept most major insurance plans including Anthem Blue Cross, Aetna, Cigna, United Healthcare, Medi-Cal, and many others. Verify your insurance for free.

Do you provide LGBTQ+-affirming treatment?

Absolutely. Our clinical team provides inclusive, culturally competent care for all patients. We understand the unique challenges facing the LGBTQ+ community and create a safe, affirming treatment environment.

Are there AA or NA meetings in Long Beach?

Yes, Long Beach has an extensive recovery community with hundreds of AA and NA meetings daily. The Long Beach Central Office (562-989-7697) can help locate meetings, including specialized meetings for young people, LGBTQ+ individuals, and Spanish-speakers.
