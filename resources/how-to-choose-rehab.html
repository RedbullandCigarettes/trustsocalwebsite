How to Choose a Rehab Center

Choosing the right treatment program is critical for successful recovery. This guide helps you know what to look for and what questions to ask.

Why Choosing Carefully Matters

Not all treatment programs are created equal. The right program can be life-changing; the wrong one can waste time and money while leaving addiction unaddressed. Taking time to evaluate your options—even when you feel urgent about getting help—is worth it.

1. Check Accreditation

Look for: Joint Commission (JCAHO), CARF, or state licensing

Accreditation means an independent body has verified that the program meets quality standards. Joint Commission accreditation is the gold standard—it requires regular inspections, qualified staff, evidence-based practices, and patient safety protocols.

2. Verify Staff Credentials

Quality treatment requires qualified professionals:

  • Medical Director: Board-certified physician (addiction medicine ideal)
  • Therapists: Licensed (LMFT, LCSW, LPC) with addiction training
  • Nursing Staff: RNs for detox and medical care
  • Psychiatrist: For medication management and dual diagnosis
  • Staff-to-Client Ratio: Lower is better for individualized care

3. Evaluate Treatment Approaches

Look for evidence-based therapies:

  • Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)
  • Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT)
  • EMDR (for trauma)
  • Motivational Enhancement Therapy
  • Family Therapy
  • Medication-Assisted Treatment (when appropriate)

Beware of: Programs that promise miracle cures, rely solely on one approach, or can't explain their treatment philosophy clearly.

4. Ask About Dual Diagnosis

Many people with addiction also have mental health conditions (depression, anxiety, PTSD, bipolar). Ask:

  • Do you have a psychiatrist on staff?
  • How do you screen for co-occurring disorders?
  • Is mental health treatment integrated or separate?

5. Understand the Continuum of Care

Quality programs offer multiple levels of care and clear transitions:

  • Medical Detox → Residential → PHP → IOP → Aftercare
  • Ask: How do you determine when someone is ready to step down?
  • Ask: What aftercare planning do you provide?

6. Review Amenities vs. Treatment

Important distinction: Luxury amenities (pools, gourmet food, beachfront location) don't make treatment more effective. Nice facilities can improve comfort, but the quality of clinical care matters most.

Focus on: therapy hours, staff credentials, treatment approaches, outcomes—not just pretty pictures.

7. Understand Costs and Insurance

Ask these questions:

  • What insurances do you accept?
  • Will you verify my benefits before admission?
  • What's included in the quoted price?
  • What costs might not be covered?
  • Do you offer payment plans?

8. Ask About Outcomes

Reputable programs should be able to discuss outcomes, though be wary of specific "success rate" claims (addiction recovery is complex and hard to measure). Ask:

  • How do you measure treatment effectiveness?
  • What percentage of clients complete treatment?
  • Do you follow up with clients after discharge?

9. Consider Location

There are advantages to both local and distant treatment:

Treatment Close to Home:

  • Easier family involvement
  • Smoother aftercare transition
  • Lower travel costs

Treatment Away from Home:

  • Distance from triggers and dealers
  • Fresh environment for fresh start
  • Privacy from local community

Red Flags to Watch For

  • Promises of guaranteed results or "cures"
  • Unwillingness to discuss treatment approaches
  • Pressure to admit immediately without assessment
  • No accreditation or licensing
  • Can't provide staff credentials
  • Hidden costs revealed after admission
  • No aftercare or discharge planning
  • One-size-fits-all approach to all clients

Questions to Ask

  1. Are you accredited? By whom?
  2. What are your staff credentials?
  3. What treatment approaches do you use?
  4. How do you treat co-occurring mental health conditions?
  5. What does a typical day look like?
  6. What's your staff-to-client ratio?
  7. How long is your program?
  8. What happens after treatment (aftercare)?
  9. Do you accept my insurance?
  10. Can I tour the facility before committing?
