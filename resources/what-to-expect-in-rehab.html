Your First Day: What Happens During Admission

The first day is often the hardestâ€”not because of what happens, but because of the unknown. Here's exactly what to expect:

Arrival & Check-In

When you arrive, a friendly staff member will greet you and help you feel welcome. You'll:

  • Complete admission paperwork (insurance, consent forms, emergency contacts)
  • Have your belongings checked for prohibited items (we'll store valuables safely)
  • Receive a tour of the facility
  • Meet your care team

Medical Assessment

A nurse and physician will complete a thorough medical evaluation:

  • Vital signs (blood pressure, heart rate, temperature)
  • Medical history review
  • Current medications
  • Substance use history (what you use, how much, how long)
  • Lab work (blood tests, drug screening)
  • Withdrawal risk assessment

Clinical Assessment

A therapist or counselor will meet with you to understand:

  • Your addiction history and previous treatment attempts
  • Mental health symptoms (depression, anxiety, trauma)
  • Family and social situation
  • Goals for treatment
  • Any immediate concerns or needs

Medical Detox: Days 1-10

If you're physically dependent on drugs or alcohol, you'll start with medical detox. This is the most intensive phase of treatment, but our medical team keeps you safe and as comfortable as possible.

What Detox Looks Like

  • 24/7 medical monitoring â€” Nurses check on you regularly, day and night
  • Comfort medications â€” FDA-approved medications reduce withdrawal symptoms
  • Private or semi-private room â€” Rest and recover in a comfortable space
  • Nutritious meals â€” Proper nutrition helps your body heal
  • Light activities â€” When you feel up to it, you can join gentle groups

Detox Timeline by Substance

Alcohol: 5-7 days. Symptoms peak around day 2-3. Medical monitoring critical.
Opioids/Heroin: 5-10 days. Symptoms peak around day 2-4. MAT often started.
Benzodiazepines: 2-8+ weeks. Gradual taper required. Never stop abruptly.
Stimulants (meth, cocaine): 3-7 days. Mainly fatigue and depression. No medical danger.

Types of Therapy You'll Experience

  • Individual therapy: One-on-one sessions with your primary therapist (2-3x/week)
  • Group therapy: Process groups, skills groups, peer support (daily)
  • Family therapy: Sessions with loved ones to heal relationships (weekly)
  • Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT): Changing thought patterns that lead to use
  • Trauma therapy: Processing past experiences that fuel addiction
  • Relapse prevention: Identifying triggers and building coping strategies

Frequently Asked Questions

What happens on the first day of rehab?

On your first day, you'll complete intake paperwork, undergo a medical assessment, meet with clinical staff, get oriented to the facility, and begin the detox process if needed. Staff will explain rules, schedules, and what to expect. Most people feel nervous at first but quickly settle in.

Can I bring my phone to rehab?

Phone policies vary by facility. At Trust SoCal, phones are typically collected during the first week to help you focus on recovery without distractions. After the initial period, supervised phone time is usually allowed for calls with approved family members.

What should I pack for rehab?

Pack comfortable clothing for 1-2 weeks, toiletries (no alcohol-based products), medications in original containers, ID and insurance cards, a journal or notebook, and family photos. Don't bring valuables, weapons, or revealing clothing. We'll provide a detailed packing list.

How long does rehab last?

Medical detox typically lasts 3-10 days. Residential treatment usually lasts 30, 60, or 90 days. Outpatient programs can last 8-12 weeks or longer. Research shows longer treatment produces better outcomes.

Can I leave rehab if I want to?

Yes, treatment is voluntary for adults. However, leaving early significantly increases relapse risk. If you're struggling, talk to your care teamâ€”they can often address your concerns and help you continue treatment.

Will I share a room?

This varies by facility and insurance. Trust SoCal offers both private and semi-private rooms. Sharing a room with a peer in recovery can actually be therapeuticâ€”you're not alone in this.
