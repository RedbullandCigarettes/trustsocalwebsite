What is a Partial Hospitalization Program?

Partial Hospitalization Program (PHP) bridges the gap between inpatient and outpatient care. You attend intensive treatment 5-7 days per week for 6-8 hours daily, but return home each evening. It's ideal for those stepping down from residential treatment or who need more structure than standard outpatient care.

Our PHP program provides the clinical intensity of inpatient care with the flexibility to sleep at home, maintain some responsibilities, and practice recovery skills in real-world settings. Many patients combine PHP with sober living homes for the best of both worlds.

Who Benefits from PHP?

Partial hospitalization is ideal for:

  • Patients transitioning from residential treatment or detox
  • Those who need intensive care but have a stable living environment
  • People with co-occurring mental health conditions requiring daily monitoring
  • Individuals who've relapsed after outpatient treatment
  • Anyone needing structure and support but unable to commit to 24/7 residential care

Frequently Asked Questions About PHP

What is a Partial Hospitalization Program (PHP)?

PHP is an intensive day treatment program where patients attend treatment 5-6 days per week for 5-6 hours daily, then return home in the evenings. It provides structured therapy and medical support similar to residential treatment while allowing patients to practice recovery skills in real-world settings.

Who is PHP treatment best for?

PHP is ideal for patients stepping down from residential treatment, those who need more support than IOP can provide, individuals with stable housing and transportation, and people who need intensive treatment but can safely return home each evening. It's also appropriate for patients with co-occurring mental health conditions.

How long does PHP treatment last?

PHP typically lasts 2-4 weeks, depending on individual progress and clinical recommendations. Some patients complete the program in as few as 10 days, while others benefit from 6+ weeks of PHP before transitioning to IOP or outpatient care.

What's the daily schedule like in PHP?

A typical PHP day runs from 9 AM to 3 PM and includes individual therapy, group therapy sessions, educational workshops, medication management, life skills training, and lunch. Patients return home in the evenings to practice skills learned during the day.

Does insurance cover PHP treatment?

Yes, most insurance plans cover PHP as a medically necessary level of care for addiction treatment. Trust SoCal is in-network with Health Net, Aetna, Blue Cross Blue Shield, Cigna, and United Healthcare. We provide free insurance verification to determine your coverage.
