What is Medical Detox?

Medical detox is the first critical step in addiction treatment. When you've been using drugs or alcohol regularly, your body becomes physically dependent. Stopping suddenly can cause dangerous withdrawal symptoms‚Äîthat's why medical supervision is essential.

At Trust SoCal's Orange County detox center, our board-certified physicians and nursing staff provide 24/7 monitoring to keep you safe and comfortable throughout withdrawal. We use FDA-approved medications to ease symptoms and ensure your safety during this challenging transition.

Frequently Asked Questions: Medical Detox

How long does medical detox take?

Medical detox typically takes 3-7 days, depending on the substance, length of use, and individual factors. Alcohol detox usually takes 5-7 days, while opioid detox may take 5-10 days. Our medical team provides a personalized timeline based on your specific situation.

Is medical detox covered by insurance?

Yes, most insurance plans cover medical detox as it's considered medically necessary treatment. We work with most major insurance providers and can verify your benefits before admission. Call (555) 123-4567 for free insurance verification.

What happens after detox is complete?

After completing detox, we work with you to develop a comprehensive treatment plan. Most patients transition to residential treatment (30-90 days), partial hospitalization (PHP), or intensive outpatient programs (IOP) to address the psychological aspects of addiction and build skills for lasting recovery.

Is medical detox safe?

Yes, medical detox at Trust SoCal is very safe. We provide 24/7 medical supervision with board-certified physicians and nursing staff. We use FDA-approved medications to manage withdrawal symptoms and monitor vital signs continuously. Attempting detox without medical supervision can be dangerous, especially for alcohol and benzodiazepines.

Can I have visitors during detox?

Yes, we encourage family support during recovery. Visiting hours are typically in the evenings, and we can arrange family meetings with our clinical team to discuss treatment progress and next steps. We also offer virtual visits when in-person isn't possible.
