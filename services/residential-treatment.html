What is Residential Treatment?

Residential treatment (also called inpatient rehab) is the gold standard for addiction recovery. You live at our facility for 30-90 days, receiving intensive therapy and medical support in a structured, drug-free environment away from triggers and daily stressors.

After completing medical detox, residential treatment addresses the psychological, emotional, and behavioral aspects of addiction. Our comprehensive program combines evidence-based therapies, medical monitoring, life skills training, and holistic wellness to give you the tools for lasting sobriety.

Who Benefits from Residential Treatment?

Residential rehab is ideal for people who:

  • Have moderate to severe addiction requiring intensive support
  • Haven't succeeded with outpatient treatment in the past
  • Have a home environment that's not supportive of recovery
  • Need a break from work, family, or other stressors to focus on healing
  • Have co-occurring mental health conditions requiring integrated treatment
  • Want the highest level of care and best chance at long-term success

Frequently Asked Questions About Residential Treatment

How long does residential treatment last?

Residential treatment at Trust SoCal typically lasts 30-90 days, depending on individual needs. Most patients benefit from 30-45 days for initial stabilization, though longer stays of 60-90 days show significantly higher success rates for lasting recovery, especially for those with co-occurring disorders or long-term addiction.

What is included in residential treatment?

Our residential program includes 24/7 medical supervision, individual therapy (3-5 sessions per week), group therapy, medication management, family counseling, holistic therapies like yoga and meditation, nutritious meals, private or semi-private accommodations, and comprehensive aftercare planning to support long-term recovery.

Can I use my phone during residential treatment?

Phone usage policies vary by treatment phase. During the first week, phone access is typically limited to help patients focus on recovery without outside distractions. After initial stabilization, scheduled phone time is allowed to maintain family connections while protecting the therapeutic environment.

Does insurance cover residential addiction treatment?

Yes, most insurance plans cover residential addiction treatment under the Mental Health Parity and Addiction Equity Act. Trust SoCal is in-network with major insurers including Health Net, Aetna, Blue Cross Blue Shield, Cigna, and United Healthcare. We offer free insurance verification to determine your exact coverage and out-of-pocket costs.

What happens after residential treatment?

After residential treatment, patients transition to a lower level of care such as Partial Hospitalization (PHP), Intensive Outpatient (IOP), or sober living. Trust SoCal provides comprehensive aftercare planning including alumni support groups, relapse prevention strategies, and continued access to our clinical team for ongoing support.
