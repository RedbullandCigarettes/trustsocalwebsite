What is an Intensive Outpatient Program?

Intensive Outpatient Program (IOP) provides structured addiction treatment while allowing you to live at home and maintain daily responsibilities. You attend therapy sessions 3-5 evenings per week (9-12 hours total) and spend the rest of your time working, going to school, or caring for family.

IOP is perfect for stepping down from partial hospitalization or residential treatment, or as your starting point if you have a stable home environment and strong support system. Most patients combine IOP with sober living for additional structure and peer support.

Who Benefits from IOP?

Intensive outpatient treatment is ideal for:

  • Individuals transitioning from PHP or higher levels of care
  • Those with mild to moderate addiction who have a supportive home environment
  • People who need to maintain employment or family responsibilities
  • Patients who want to practice recovery skills in real-world settings
  • Anyone seeking structured support without 24/7 supervision

Frequently Asked Questions About IOP

What is the difference between IOP and PHP?

IOP (Intensive Outpatient) requires 9-12 hours of treatment per week, typically in evening sessions 3-4 days per week. PHP (Partial Hospitalization) is more intensive at 20-30 hours per week, usually 5-6 days. IOP allows you to work full-time, while PHP is designed for those needing more support during the day.

Can I work while in IOP treatment?

Yes! IOP is specifically designed for working professionals. Our evening sessions (6-9 PM) allow you to maintain your job, attend school, or care for family during the day while receiving intensive addiction treatment in the evenings.

How long does IOP treatment last?

Most IOP programs last 8-12 weeks, depending on individual progress and needs. Some patients complete IOP in as few as 6 weeks, while others benefit from extended treatment of 16+ weeks. Your treatment team will work with you to determine the optimal duration.

Does insurance cover IOP programs?

Yes, most insurance plans cover Intensive Outpatient Programs as a medically necessary level of addiction treatment. Trust SoCal accepts Aetna, Blue Cross Blue Shield, Cigna, United Healthcare, Health Net, and most PPO and HMO plans. We offer free insurance verification.

What if I relapse during IOP?

Relapse during IOP is addressed with compassion, not judgment. If you experience a setback, we'll work with you to adjust your treatment plan, which may include increased session frequency, individual counseling, or temporarily stepping up to PHP. Relapse can be part of recovery - what matters is getting back on track quickly.
