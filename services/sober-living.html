What is Sober Living?

Sober living homes (also called halfway houses or recovery residences) provide structured, alcohol and drug-free housing for people in early recovery. They bridge the gap between inpatient treatment and independent living, offering a supportive environment to practice recovery skills.

Unlike treatment facilities, sober living homes are residential settings where you live with other people in recovery. While attending IOP or PHP, you return to a safe, sober environment each evening. Most residents stay 3-12 months as they build a foundation for long-term sobriety.

Who Benefits from Sober Living?

Sober living is ideal for:

  • Individuals completing residential treatment or detox who aren't ready to return home
  • Those attending PHP or IOP who need structured housing
  • People whose home environment isn't supportive of recovery
  • Anyone wanting to build a sober community and accountability network
  • Individuals rebuilding their lives after addiction who need time and stability

House Rules & Expectations

Our sober living homes maintain clear structure and expectations to support everyone's recovery:

Requirements

  • Complete abstinence from alcohol and drugs
  • Active participation in IOP, PHP, or aftercare programming
  • Attend 12-step meetings or other recovery support groups
  • Employment or active job searching within 30 days
  • Random drug and alcohol testing compliance
  • Respect curfew and house quiet hours
  • Complete assigned chores and maintain cleanliness
  • Participate in weekly house meetings
  • Treat all residents and staff with respect

What's Included

  • Furnished bedroom (private or shared options)
  • All utilities (electricity, water, internet, cable)
  • Kitchen access and communal meals
  • Laundry facilities
  • Transportation assistance to treatment/work
  • 24/7 house manager support
  • Weekly house meetings and activities
