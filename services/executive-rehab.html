Understanding Executive Addiction

Professionals often develop substance use disorders differently than the general population. High-achievers may use alcohol or drugs to manage stress, maintain energy, cope with performance anxiety, or simply decompress from demanding schedules. Common patterns we treat include:

Alcohol Dependence

Business dinners, networking events, and "unwinding" after work can escalate to dependency. Many executives maintain high functioning despite severe alcohol addiction.

Prescription Drug Misuse

Stimulants for focus, benzodiazepines for anxiety, opioids for chronic pain—prescription medications can become problematic even when initially prescribed legitimately.

Cocaine & Stimulants

Cocaine and other stimulants are prevalent in high-pressure industries where long hours and mental sharpness are expected.

Co-Occurring Conditions

Many executives also struggle with anxiety, depression, burnout, or trauma that requires integrated dual diagnosis treatment.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will my employer find out I'm in rehab?

No. Trust SoCal maintains strict HIPAA compliance and confidentiality. We never contact employers without explicit written permission. Many executives use FMLA leave, personal leave, or flexible work arrangements. Our admissions team can help you plan a discreet approach.

Can I continue working during executive rehab?

Limited work accommodations are available depending on your treatment level. Our executive program offers private spaces for confidential calls, Wi-Fi access during designated hours, and flexible scheduling for critical business matters. However, treatment remains the priority during your stay.

What makes executive rehab different from regular treatment?

Executive rehab provides private or semi-private accommodations, smaller therapy group sizes, work accommodations, enhanced confidentiality protocols, peer groups with other professionals, and treatment for high-pressure career issues like burnout, performance anxiety, and work-related trauma.

Does insurance cover executive addiction treatment?

Yes, most insurance plans cover addiction treatment including executive programs. Trust SoCal is in-network with Health Net, Aetna, Blue Cross Blue Shield, Cigna, and United Healthcare. We offer confidential insurance verification so your employer is never contacted.

How long is executive rehab?

Executive treatment typically ranges from 30-90 days depending on individual needs. Many professionals benefit from 30-45 days of residential treatment followed by outpatient care that accommodates work schedules. We create personalized treatment plans based on your career demands and recovery needs.
