Does Cigna Cover Drug and Alcohol Rehab?

Yes, Cigna provides coverage for drug and alcohol addiction treatment. Through Cigna Behavioral Health, members have access to substance abuse treatment benefits as required by the Mental Health Parity and Addiction Equity Act.

Cigna Addiction Treatment Coverage Includes:

  • Medical Detoxification: 24/7 supervised withdrawal management
  • Residential/Inpatient Treatment: Coverage based on medical necessity
  • Partial Hospitalization Program (PHP): Day treatment programs
  • Intensive Outpatient Program (IOP): Flexible treatment options
  • Outpatient Therapy: Individual and group counseling
  • Medication-Assisted Treatment (MAT): Suboxone, Vivitrol prescriptions

Frequently Asked Questions About Cigna Rehab Coverage

Does Cigna cover drug rehab?

Yes, Cigna covers drug and alcohol rehab as part of their behavioral health benefits. Coverage includes medical detox, residential treatment, partial hospitalization (PHP), and intensive outpatient programs (IOP). Cigna Behavioral Health manages substance abuse treatment authorization.

How much does Cigna pay for rehab?

Cigna typically covers 70-80% of in-network addiction treatment costs after the deductible. Out-of-pocket costs vary based on your specific plan, deductible, copay, and out-of-pocket maximum. Trust SoCal offers free insurance verification to determine your exact coverage.

Does Cigna require pre-authorization for rehab?

Yes, most Cigna plans require pre-authorization through Cigna Behavioral Health before starting addiction treatment. Trust SoCal handles all pre-authorization on your behalf, working directly with Cigna to obtain approval quickly.

How do I use my Cigna insurance for rehab?

Simply call Trust SoCal at (555) 123-4567 with your Cigna member ID. We handle all insurance verification and pre-authorization. Our team works directly with Cigna Behavioral Health to confirm coverage and start your treatment quickly.

What if Cigna denies my treatment?

If Cigna denies coverage, Trust SoCal's billing department files appeals on your behalf. We understand Cigna's criteria and documentation requirements. Many initial denials are overturned on appeal—don't let a denial stop you from getting help.
