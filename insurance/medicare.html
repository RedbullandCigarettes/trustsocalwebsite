Medicare Addiction Treatment Benefits

Medicare provides coverage for substance use disorder treatment. Whether you have Original Medicare (Parts A & B) or a Medicare Advantage plan, you likely have benefits for addiction treatment services including detox, rehabilitation, and outpatient care.

Addiction doesn't discriminate by age. Older adults face unique addiction challenges—chronic pain leading to opioid dependence, loss of spouse or retirement triggering alcohol abuse, long-hidden addictions finally reaching crisis. You deserve treatment that understands these realities.

Medicare Coverage Overview

  • Part A (Hospital Insurance): Inpatient detox and residential treatment
  • Part B (Medical Insurance): Outpatient treatment, doctor visits, therapy
  • Part D (Prescription Drugs): Medications used in addiction treatment
  • Medicare Advantage (Part C): Varies by plan; often includes additional benefits

Covered Treatment Services

Medicare Part A Covers:

  • Inpatient medical detoxification
  • Inpatient residential rehabilitation
  • Hospital-based treatment programs
  • Medically necessary inpatient services

Medicare Part B Covers:

  • Partial hospitalization programs (PHP)
  • Intensive outpatient programs (IOP)
  • Individual and group therapy
  • Psychiatric services
  • Medication management
  • Screening and assessment

Cost Sharing

For Original Medicare:

  • Part A: Deductible per benefit period; coinsurance after day 60
  • Part B: 20% coinsurance after meeting annual deductible

If you have Medigap (supplemental insurance), it may cover some or all of your out-of-pocket costs.

Addiction in Older Adults

Substance use disorders in older adults are often overlooked or misdiagnosed. Common patterns include:

  • Prescription opioid dependence: Starting with legitimate pain management
  • Alcohol use disorder: Increasing consumption after retirement or loss
  • Benzodiazepine dependence: Long-term use for sleep or anxiety
  • Late-onset addiction: Developing problems after decades without issues
  • Interaction risks: Substances interacting with medications

Age-Appropriate Care

Trust SoCal provides treatment appropriate for older adults:

  • Medical team experienced with older adults' health needs
  • Careful medication management considering multiple prescriptions
  • Pace appropriate to individual energy and ability levels
  • Addressing loss, grief, isolation, and life transitions
  • Coordination with primary care providers
  • Respect for life experience and dignity

Medicare Advantage Plans

If you have a Medicare Advantage (Part C) plan, your addiction treatment benefits may differ from Original Medicare. Many Advantage plans offer:

  • Lower out-of-pocket costs
  • Additional covered services
  • Network requirements (we work with many plans)
  • Prior authorization requirements

Call us to verify your specific plan's benefits.
