Proven Approaches

Evidence-Based Treatment Modalities

Our treatment programs integrate the most effective, scientifically validated therapies proven to support lasting recovery from substance use disorders.

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)

CBT helps you identify and change destructive thought patterns that lead to addictive behaviors. Through structured sessions, you'll learn to recognize triggers, challenge negative thinking, and develop healthier coping strategies that support long-term sobriety.

Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT)

DBT teaches crucial skills for managing intense emotions without turning to substances. You'll learn mindfulness, distress tolerance, emotional regulation, and interpersonal effectiveness‚Äîall essential tools for navigating life's challenges in recovery.

EMDR for Trauma

Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing (EMDR) is highly effective for treating underlying trauma that often contributes to addiction. This therapy helps process traumatic memories and reduce their emotional impact, allowing you to heal at the deepest level.

Motivational Interviewing

This client-centered approach strengthens your personal motivation for change. Rather than confrontation, our therapists use collaborative conversation to help you explore your own reasons for recovery and build commitment to lasting change.

Medication-Assisted Treatment (MAT)

For opioid and alcohol dependence, FDA-approved medications like buprenorphine, naltrexone, and acamprosate can reduce cravings and withdrawal symptoms. Combined with therapy, MAT significantly improves treatment outcomes and reduces relapse risk.

12-Step Facilitation

We help you engage with 12-Step recovery communities like AA and NA, which provide powerful peer support and spiritual principles for recovery. Our staff facilitates meeting attendance, step work, and sponsor connections to build your recovery network.

Family Therapy

Addiction affects the whole family. Our family therapy sessions heal damaged relationships, establish healthy boundaries, and educate loved ones about addiction and recovery. Strong family support is one of the best predictors of long-term success.

Dual Diagnosis Treatment

Many individuals struggle with co-occurring mental health conditions like depression, anxiety, PTSD, or bipolar disorder alongside addiction. Our integrated approach treats both conditions simultaneously for complete, lasting healing.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions About Treatment

Get answers to common questions about our addiction treatment programs in Orange County.

How long does addiction treatment take?

Treatment length varies by individual needs. Medical detox typically takes 5-10 days. Residential treatment is usually 30-90 days. Partial hospitalization (PHP) lasts 2-4 weeks. Intensive outpatient (IOP) runs 8-12 weeks. Research shows longer treatment produces better outcomes‚Äîwe recommend stepping through levels of care rather than rushing to complete treatment.

What's the difference between residential and outpatient treatment?

Residential treatment provides 24/7 care where you live at our facility‚Äîbest for severe addiction, unstable home environments, or co-occurring mental health disorders. Outpatient programs allow you to live at home while attending treatment sessions‚Äîbetter for mild-to-moderate addiction with strong support systems. Our clinical team recommends the appropriate level based on your individual assessment.

Do I need detox before treatment?

It depends on the substance and how recently you used. If you're physically dependent on alcohol, opioids, or benzodiazepines, medical detox is typically necessary before starting therapy. Detox from alcohol and benzodiazepines can be life-threatening without medical supervision. Our admissions team will assess your needs and recommend the appropriate starting point.

What therapies do you use in treatment?

We use evidence-based therapies proven effective for addiction treatment: Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT), EMDR for trauma, Motivational Interviewing, 12-Step Facilitation, and family therapy. We also offer holistic therapies including yoga, meditation, art therapy, and fitness programming. Treatment plans are individualized‚Äîwe use the approaches that work best for your specific situation.

Can I continue working during treatment?

Residential treatment requires taking time away from work. PHP is typically too intensive for full-time employment. IOP is specifically designed to accommodate work schedules, with sessions often in evenings. Many employers are required to hold your job during treatment under FMLA. Your recovery is worth the investment‚Äîand you can't work effectively while struggling with active addiction.

What happens after I complete treatment?

Treatment doesn't end at discharge. Our aftercare program provides ongoing support including alumni groups, individual therapy, and access to support meetings. Many clients transition to sober living for continued structure. We also help connect you with community resources like AA/NA meetings in Santa Ana, Irvine, Costa Mesa, and throughout Orange County.
