Non-Discrimination Policy

Everyone Deserves Access to Quality Addiction Treatment

Trust SoCal is committed to providing equal access to quality addiction treatment services for all individuals, regardless of background.

Our Commitment to Equal Access

Trust SoCal complies with applicable Federal civil rights laws and does not discriminate on the basis of race, color, national origin, age, disability, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity, or religion. We do not exclude people or treat them differently because of these characteristics.

This policy applies to all aspects of our services, including but not limited to admission, treatment, discharge, access to facilities, and interactions with staff.

Legal Compliance

Trust SoCal complies with the following federal and state laws:

  • Title VI of the Civil Rights Act of 1964 - Prohibits discrimination based on race, color, or national origin
  • Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act of 1973 - Prohibits discrimination based on disability
  • Age Discrimination Act of 1975 - Prohibits discrimination based on age
  • Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) - Ensures accessibility and accommodation for individuals with disabilities
  • Section 1557 of the Affordable Care Act - Prohibits discrimination in healthcare settings
  • California Fair Employment and Housing Act - State protections against discrimination
  • California Unruh Civil Rights Act - Ensures full and equal accommodations

Accessibility Services

Trust SoCal provides the following services to ensure equal access:

Language Assistance

Free language services for people whose primary language is not English, including qualified interpreters and information written in other languages. Spanish language services are readily available.

Disability Accommodations

Reasonable modifications and auxiliary aids for people with disabilities, including accessible facilities, sign language interpreters, and materials in alternative formats.

Communication Assistance

TTY/TDD services, large print materials, and other communication aids for individuals with hearing or vision impairments.

If you need these services, please contact us at (555) 123-4567.
