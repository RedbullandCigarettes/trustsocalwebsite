Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions About Our Team

What credentials do your addiction treatment staff have?

Our team includes board-certified physicians in addiction medicine and psychiatry, Licensed Clinical Social Workers (LCSW), Licensed Marriage and Family Therapists (LMFT), Certified Alcohol and Drug Counselors (CADC), Registered Nurses (RN), and certified peer support specialists. All clinical staff maintain active licenses and complete ongoing education.

Do you have medical staff available 24/7?

Yes. Our residential treatment programs have licensed nursing staff on-site 24/7 with physician oversight. Medical emergencies can be addressed immediately, and our detox program provides round-the-clock medical monitoring to ensure patient safety and comfort.

What is the staff-to-client ratio at Trust SoCal?

We maintain a low staff-to-client ratio of approximately 1:4 in our residential program, ensuring personalized attention and care. Group therapy sessions typically have 8-12 participants with two facilitators, while individual therapy is one-on-one with your assigned therapist.

Do any staff members have personal recovery experience?

Many of our team members are in long-term recovery themselves, bringing lived experience to complement their professional training. Our peer support specialists have a minimum of 2 years in recovery and provide mentorship, hope, and practical guidance based on their own recovery journeys.

How do you match clients with therapists?

During intake assessment, we consider each client's specific needs, preferences, and treatment goals to match them with the most appropriate therapist. Factors include the type of addiction, co-occurring disorders, trauma history, and communication style. Clients can request a different therapist if they feel the match isn't right.
