Understanding Alcohol Use Disorder

Alcohol use disorder (AUD) is a chronic brain disease characterized by compulsive alcohol use, loss of control over intake, and negative emotional states during withdrawal. According to the National Institute on Alcohol Abuse and Alcoholism (NIAAA), approximately 14.5 million Americans ages 12 and older have AUD.

Signs and Symptoms of Alcoholism

  • Inability to limit alcohol consumption
  • Strong cravings or urges to drink
  • Drinking interferes with work, school, or relationships
  • Continued drinking despite physical or psychological problems
  • Needing more alcohol to feel its effects (tolerance)
  • Experiencing withdrawal symptoms when not drinking
  • Spending significant time drinking or recovering from drinking
  • Giving up activities you once enjoyed to drink

Health Consequences of Alcohol Abuse

Chronic alcohol abuse can lead to serious health complications including:

  • Liver disease: Fatty liver, alcoholic hepatitis, cirrhosis
  • Cardiovascular problems: High blood pressure, cardiomyopathy, stroke
  • Neurological damage: Memory problems, cognitive impairment, neuropathy
  • Mental health: Depression, anxiety, increased suicide risk
  • Digestive issues: Pancreatitis, gastritis, ulcers
  • Immune system: Increased susceptibility to infections

Our Alcohol Addiction Treatment Programs

Medical Detox (5-7 Days)

Alcohol withdrawal can be dangerous and even life-threatening. Our medical detox program provides 24/7 supervision with:

  • Board-certified addiction medicine physicians
  • FDA-approved medications to manage withdrawal symptoms
  • Round-the-clock nursing care and monitoring
  • Comfortable private rooms
  • Nutritional support and hydration therapy

Residential Treatment (30-90 Days)

After detox, our residential program addresses the psychological aspects of alcohol addiction:

  • Individual therapy with licensed counselors
  • Group therapy and peer support
  • Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)
  • Motivational Enhancement Therapy (MET)
  • Family therapy and education
  • Relapse prevention skills training
  • 12-step and alternative recovery programs

Outpatient Programs (IOP/PHP)

For those who need flexibility, our outpatient programs offer:

  • Partial Hospitalization Program (PHP): 5-6 days/week, 6+ hours/day
  • Intensive Outpatient Program (IOP): 3-5 days/week, 3-4 hours/day
  • Evening and weekend options available
  • Continued therapy and support
  • Medication management

Medication-Assisted Treatment (MAT)

We use FDA-approved medications as part of comprehensive treatment:

  • Naltrexone (Vivitrol): Reduces cravings and blocks alcohol's rewarding effects
  • Acamprosate (Campral): Helps maintain abstinence by reducing withdrawal-related anxiety
  • Disulfiram (Antabuse): Creates unpleasant effects if alcohol is consumed

Frequently Asked Questions About Alcohol Rehab

How long does alcohol detox take?

Alcohol detox typically takes 5-7 days, though symptoms may persist for 2-3 weeks. Medical supervision is critical as alcohol withdrawal can be life-threatening. At Trust SoCal, we provide 24/7 medical monitoring to ensure safe, comfortable detoxification.

What are the symptoms of alcohol withdrawal?

Alcohol withdrawal symptoms include anxiety, tremors, sweating, nausea, insomnia, and in severe cases, seizures and delirium tremens (DTs). Symptoms typically begin 6-12 hours after the last drink and peak around 24-72 hours. Medical detox helps manage these symptoms safely.

Does insurance cover alcohol rehab?

Yes, most insurance plans cover alcohol addiction treatment under mental health and substance abuse benefits. The Mental Health Parity Act requires insurers to cover addiction treatment similarly to other medical conditions. Trust SoCal accepts most major insurance providers and offers free verification.

What medications are used for alcohol addiction treatment?

FDA-approved medications for alcohol use disorder include Naltrexone (reduces cravings), Acamprosate (helps maintain abstinence), and Disulfiram (creates unpleasant effects if alcohol is consumed). Our physicians determine the best medication based on individual needs.
