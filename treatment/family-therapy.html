Why Family Involvement Matters

Research consistently shows that family involvement dramatically improves addiction treatment outcomes. When families participate in treatment, clients are more likely to complete programs, maintain sobriety longer, and rebuild the relationships that matter most.

Addiction is often called a "family disease" because it affects everyone connected to the person struggling. Family members experience their own trauma, develop unhealthy coping patterns, and need support too. Our family program addresses the whole system, not just the individual.

How Addiction Affects Families

  • Broken Trust: Lies, broken promises, and betrayals damage the foundation of relationships
  • Role Confusion: Family members take on unhealthy roles (enabler, hero, scapegoat)
  • Communication Breakdown: Families stop talking openly and honestly
  • Financial Strain: Addiction often creates serious financial problems
  • Emotional Exhaustion: Years of worry, anger, and disappointment take a toll
  • Codependency: Patterns of enabling and over-functioning develop

Our Family Program

Family Education Sessions

Learn about addiction as a disease, how it affects the brain, and what recovery really looks like. Understanding addiction helps families move from blame and frustration toward support and compassion.

Family Therapy Sessions

Regular therapy sessions with your loved one and a trained family therapist. Work through past hurts, practice healthy communication, and begin rebuilding trust in a safe, facilitated environment.

Multi-Family Groups

Connect with other families going through similar experiences. There's powerful healing in knowing you're not alone and learning from families further along in the process.

Communication Skills Training

Learn practical skills for healthy communication: setting boundaries, expressing needs without attacking, active listening, and navigating difficult conversations about addiction and recovery.

Codependency Work

Family members often develop codependent patterns in response to addiction. Learn to recognize these patterns, set healthy boundaries, and focus on your own recovery alongside your loved one's.

Family Therapy Approaches We Use

Evidence-Based Family Therapies

  • Behavioral Couples Therapy (BCT): For couples where one partner has a substance use disorder
  • Family Behavior Therapy (FBT): Addresses family dynamics and individual behaviors
  • Multidimensional Family Therapy (MDFT): Especially effective for adolescents and young adults
  • CRAFT Approach: Community Reinforcement and Family Training for concerned family members
  • Systemic Family Therapy: Views addiction within the context of family system patterns

What Families Learn

  • How to support recovery without enabling
  • Setting and maintaining healthy boundaries
  • Recognizing warning signs of relapse
  • Effective communication strategies
  • Self-care and managing your own stress
  • Creating a recovery-supportive home environment
  • When and how to use tough love
  • Finding support resources (Al-Anon, Nar-Anon, family support groups)

For Parents of Adults in Treatment

Watching your adult child struggle with addiction is heartbreaking. You may feel guilty, wondering what you did wrong. You may feel helpless, unable to "fix" things like you could when they were young. Our family program helps parents:

  • Move beyond guilt and blame
  • Understand healthy support vs. enabling
  • Navigate financial boundaries
  • Rebuild relationship with your adult child
  • Take care of your own wellbeing

For Spouses & Partners

Living with a partner's addiction often feels like living with a stranger. Trust has been broken. You've been lied to, disappointed, and hurt. You may be questioning whether the relationship can survive. Our couples work addresses:

  • Rebuilding trust after addiction
  • Healing from betrayal and broken promises
  • Learning to communicate honestly again
  • Addressing codependency patterns
  • Deciding whether to stay together
  • Creating a new, healthier relationship

Family Program Schedule

Our family programming includes:

  • Weekly Family Therapy: Sessions with your loved one and therapist
  • Family Education Group: Weekly educational sessions for family members
  • Multi-Family Group: Monthly sessions with other families
  • Family Weekends: Intensive family programming opportunities
  • Aftercare Planning: Preparing for continued family recovery after treatment

Getting Involved

Family participation is encouraged but never forced. We understand that family situations are complex—some family members may not be ready or able to participate, and some relationships may be too damaged to include in treatment. Our clinical team will work with you to determine the appropriate level of family involvement for your situation.
