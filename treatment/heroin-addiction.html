Understanding Heroin Addiction

Heroin is a highly addictive opioid that creates intense euphoria followed by devastating physical and psychological dependence. According to the National Institute on Drug Abuse, nearly 80% of people who use heroin first misused prescription opioids. The path to heroin addiction often begins with legitimate pain management that evolves into dependency.

How Heroin Addiction Develops

  • Rapid tolerance: Users quickly need more heroin to achieve the same effect
  • Physical dependence: The body adapts and requires heroin to function normally
  • Powerful cravings: Brain chemistry changes drive compulsive drug-seeking behavior
  • Painful withdrawal: Stopping use causes severe flu-like symptoms

Signs of Heroin Addiction

  • Track marks or needle marks on arms, legs, or other body parts
  • Constricted "pinpoint" pupils
  • Nodding off or extreme drowsiness
  • Significant weight loss and neglected appearance
  • Financial problems or stealing to fund drug use
  • Social withdrawal and secretive behavior
  • Paraphernalia: needles, spoons, rubber tubing, small bags
  • Skin infections or abscesses at injection sites

Fentanyl Warning

Today's heroin supply is frequently laced with fentanyl, which is 50-100 times more potent than heroin. This has dramatically increased overdose deaths. If you or a loved one is using heroin, seeking treatment now could save a life.

Our Heroin Addiction Treatment Programs

Medical Detox (5-10 Days)

Heroin withdrawal is intensely uncomfortable but rarely dangerous with proper medical care. Our medical detox program provides:

  • 24/7 medical supervision by addiction medicine specialists
  • MAT induction (Suboxone) to eliminate withdrawal symptoms
  • Comfort medications for nausea, muscle aches, and insomnia
  • IV fluids and nutritional support
  • Private rooms and compassionate nursing care

Our medical detox makes heroin withdrawal manageable, setting the foundation for lasting recovery.

Medication-Assisted Treatment (MAT)

MAT is the most effective treatment for heroin addiction, combining medications with therapy:

  • Suboxone (buprenorphine/naloxone): Eliminates cravings and withdrawal; can be taken at home
  • Vivitrol (naltrexone): Monthly injection that blocks opioid effects completely
  • Sublocade: Monthly buprenorphine injection for steady medication levels

Research from SAMHSA shows MAT reduces opioid overdose deaths by 50% and significantly improves treatment outcomes.

Residential Treatment (30-90 Days)

Our inpatient program provides intensive support after detox:

  • Individual therapy addressing underlying trauma and mental health
  • Group therapy and peer support
  • Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) for addiction
  • MAT continuation and monitoring
  • Life skills training and relapse prevention
  • Family therapy and education

Outpatient Programs (IOP/PHP)

Step-down care for continued support:

  • Partial Hospitalization (PHP): 5-6 days/week, 6+ hours/day
  • Intensive Outpatient (IOP): 3-5 days/week, 3-4 hours/day
  • MAT management and prescription monitoring
  • Ongoing therapy and 12-step integration

Heroin Withdrawal Timeline

Understanding what to expect during heroin detox can help prepare for treatment:

6-12 Hours: Early Withdrawal

Anxiety, muscle aches, agitation, insomnia, runny nose, sweating, yawning

Days 1-3: Peak Symptoms

Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal cramps, dilated pupils, goosebumps, intense cravings

Days 4-7: Subsiding Physical Symptoms

Physical symptoms decrease, fatigue and weakness persist, depression and cravings continue

Weeks to Months: Post-Acute Withdrawal (PAWS)

Anxiety, depression, sleep disturbances, mood swings, intermittent cravings

With medical detox and MAT, most of these symptoms can be significantly reduced or eliminated.

Frequently Asked Questions About Heroin Treatment

How long does heroin withdrawal last?

Acute heroin withdrawal typically begins 6-12 hours after the last dose and peaks within 1-3 days. Physical symptoms usually subside within 5-7 days. However, psychological symptoms and cravings can persist for weeks or months. Medical detox with MAT significantly reduces withdrawal severity.

Is heroin detox dangerous?

While heroin withdrawal is extremely uncomfortable, it is rarely life-threatening with proper medical supervision. However, complications like severe dehydration and aspiration can occur. Medical detox provides 24/7 monitoring and medications to ensure safety and comfort during withdrawal.

What medications are used for heroin addiction treatment?

FDA-approved medications for heroin addiction include Suboxone (buprenorphine/naloxone), Vivitrol (naltrexone), and methadone. These medications reduce cravings, prevent withdrawal, and help maintain long-term recovery. At Trust SoCal, our physicians determine the best medication approach for each patient.

Can I detox from heroin at home?

While technically possible, home heroin detox is not recommended. Without medical support, the intense discomfort often leads to relapse within hours. Worse, returning to heroin use after any period of abstinence significantly increases overdose risk due to reduced tolerance. Medical detox is always the safest option.

How long is heroin addiction treatment?

Treatment length varies by individual needs. Medical detox typically lasts 5-10 days, followed by 30-90 days of residential treatment. Many patients continue with outpatient programs and MAT for 6-12 months or longer. Research shows longer treatment duration correlates with better outcomes.
