Understanding Opioid Use Disorder

The opioid epidemic has devastated communities across America, with over 80,000 opioid overdose deaths in 2021 alone according to the CDC. Opioid use disorder (OUD) affects people from all backgrounds and often begins with legitimate prescriptions for pain management.

Types of Opioids We Treat

  • Prescription opioids: OxyContin, Vicodin, Percocet, morphine, codeine
  • Synthetic opioids: Fentanyl, tramadol
  • Illicit opioids: Heroin, street fentanyl

Signs of Opioid Addiction

  • Taking opioids in larger amounts or longer than intended
  • Unsuccessful efforts to cut down or control use
  • Spending significant time obtaining, using, or recovering from opioids
  • Intense cravings for opioids
  • Failing to fulfill obligations at work, school, or home
  • Continuing use despite relationship problems
  • Giving up important activities due to opioid use
  • Using opioids in physically hazardous situations
  • Developing tolerance (needing more for same effect)
  • Experiencing withdrawal symptoms when stopping

Our Opioid Addiction Treatment Programs

Medication-Assisted Treatment (MAT)

MAT is the gold standard for opioid addiction treatment, combining FDA-approved medications with counseling:

  • Suboxone (buprenorphine/naloxone): Reduces cravings and withdrawal symptoms; can be taken at home
  • Vivitrol (naltrexone): Monthly injection that blocks opioid effects; ideal after detox
  • Sublocade: Monthly buprenorphine injection for consistent medication levels

Research shows MAT reduces overdose deaths by 50% and improves treatment retention.

Medical Detox (5-10 Days)

Our medical detox makes opioid withdrawal manageable:

  • 24/7 medical supervision and monitoring
  • Comfort medications for withdrawal symptoms
  • MAT induction to ease transition
  • Private rooms and compassionate care

Residential Treatment (30-90 Days)

Our inpatient program provides intensive support:

  • Individual and group therapy
  • Trauma-informed care (many opioid users have trauma history)
  • Dual diagnosis treatment for co-occurring mental health conditions
  • MAT continuation and monitoring
  • Life skills and relapse prevention

Outpatient Programs (IOP/PHP)

Flexible treatment while maintaining daily responsibilities:

  • Partial Hospitalization (PHP): 5-6 days/week, 6+ hours/day
  • Intensive Outpatient (IOP): 3-5 days/week, 3-4 hours/day
  • MAT management and medication monitoring
  • Ongoing therapy and peer support

Frequently Asked Questions About Opioid Treatment

What is Medication-Assisted Treatment (MAT)?

MAT combines FDA-approved medications (Suboxone, Vivitrol, or methadone) with counseling and behavioral therapies. Research shows MAT reduces opioid use, overdose deaths, and criminal activity while increasing treatment retention. At Trust SoCal, our physicians determine the best MAT approach for each patient.

How long does opioid withdrawal last?

Acute opioid withdrawal typically lasts 5-10 days, with symptoms peaking around days 2-3. However, post-acute withdrawal symptoms (PAWS) such as anxiety, depression, and cravings can persist for weeks or months. Medical detox with MAT significantly reduces withdrawal discomfort.

Is Suboxone treatment safe?

Yes, Suboxone (buprenorphine/naloxone) is FDA-approved and considered safe when prescribed and monitored by qualified physicians. It reduces cravings, prevents withdrawal symptoms, and blocks other opioids from producing euphoria. Studies show long-term MAT improves outcomes significantly.

Will I be on Suboxone forever?

Treatment duration varies by individual. Some patients use MAT short-term during early recovery, while others benefit from long-term maintenance. Research supports both approaches. Our physicians work with each patient to determine the optimal treatment plan and support gradual tapering when appropriate.
