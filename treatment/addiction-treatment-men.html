Understanding Men's Addiction

Men are more likely to use drugs and alcohol, but less likely to seek treatment. At Trust SoCal, we understand that asking for help takes real courage. Our men's programming creates a judgment-free environment where you can be honest about your struggles and learn from other men who understand what you're going through.

Unique Factors in Men's Addiction

  • Stigma of Weakness: Cultural pressure to "man up" prevents many men from seeking help
  • Emotional Suppression: Many men use substances to numb emotions they were never taught to process
  • Work Pressure: Career stress and the pressure to provide often fuels substance use
  • Anger Issues: Unprocessed anger and aggression commonly co-occur with addiction
  • Risk-Taking: Men more likely to use dangerous quantities and combinations
  • Legal Consequences: Higher rates of DUI, drug offenses, and legal involvement

Our Men's Programming

Men-Only Groups

Process groups exclusively for men where you can speak openly about issues like anger, sexuality, relationships, fatherhood, and career pressure without worry about how you're perceived.

Emotional Intelligence Training

Many men never learned healthy ways to identify and express emotions. We teach practical skills for emotional awareness, communication, and coping without substances.

Anger Management

Learn to understand and manage anger in healthy ways. Our anger management component helps you recognize triggers, develop coping strategies, and express frustration constructively.

Relationship & Family Work

Address how addiction has affected your relationships with partners, children, parents, and friends. Learn communication skills and how to rebuild trust.

Career & Purpose

For many men, identity is closely tied to work. We help you navigate career concerns during and after treatment, and find purpose and meaning in recovery.

Common Substances Among Men

  • Alcohol - Often normalized in male social and work settings
  • Opioids - Including prescription painkillers, heroin, and fentanyl
  • Cocaine - Common in high-pressure professional environments
  • Methamphetamine - Increasing use across demographics
  • Steroids - Performance and image-enhancing drugs
