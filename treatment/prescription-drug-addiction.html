Understanding Prescription Drug Addiction

According to the National Institute on Drug Abuse, an estimated 18 million people misused prescription medications in the past year. Prescription drug addiction often develops gradually from legitimate medical use, making it difficult for people to recognize when use has become problematic.

Categories of Commonly Abused Prescription Drugs

Prescription Opioids (Painkillers)

OxyContin, Vicodin, Percocet, Norco, morphine, codeine

Treatment: Medical detox with MAT (Suboxone, Vivitrol)

Benzodiazepines (Anti-Anxiety/Sedatives)

Xanax, Valium, Klonopin, Ativan

Treatment: Medical tapering protocol (never stop abruptly)

Prescription Stimulants

Adderall, Ritalin, Concerta, Vyvanse

Treatment: Medical supervision for crash symptoms, behavioral therapies

Sleep Medications

Ambien, Lunesta, Sonata

Treatment: Gradual tapering with sleep hygiene education

Signs of Prescription Drug Addiction

  • Taking more medication than prescribed
  • Running out of prescriptions early
  • Visiting multiple doctors for prescriptions (doctor shopping)
  • Using medications for non-medical reasons (to get high, relax, sleep)
  • Crushing, snorting, or injecting pills
  • Defensive when questioned about medication use
  • Hiding or lying about how much you take
  • Feeling unable to function without the medication
  • Experiencing withdrawal symptoms between doses
  • Continued use despite negative consequences

Our Prescription Drug Addiction Treatment Programs

Treatment varies based on the type of prescription drug. Our medical team creates individualized protocols for safe detox and lasting recovery.

Medical Detox

Safe, medically supervised withdrawal management tailored to your specific medication:

  • Opioids: 5-10 days with MAT induction (Suboxone)
  • Benzodiazepines: 2-8+ weeks with gradual tapering (never cold turkey)
  • Stimulants: 3-7 days with supportive care for crash symptoms
  • Sleep medications: 1-2 weeks with gradual dose reduction

All detox protocols include 24/7 medical monitoring, comfort medications, and psychiatric support.

Addressing Underlying Conditions

Prescription drug addiction often starts with treating a legitimate condition. We address both:

  • Pain management: Non-addictive alternatives, physical therapy, nerve blocks
  • Anxiety: CBT, SSRIs, non-addictive medications, mindfulness
  • ADHD: Non-stimulant medications (Strattera), behavioral strategies
  • Sleep disorders: Sleep hygiene, CBT for insomnia, non-addictive options

Residential Treatment (30-90 Days)

Our inpatient program addresses the psychological aspects of prescription drug addiction:

  • Individual therapy to process shame and denial
  • Group therapy with peers who understand prescription drug struggles
  • Dual diagnosis treatment for co-occurring conditions
  • Education about addiction as a disease
  • Alternative pain and anxiety management strategies
  • Family education and therapy

Outpatient Programs

  • Partial Hospitalization (PHP): 5-6 days/week intensive treatment
  • Intensive Outpatient (IOP): 3-5 days/week flexible scheduling
  • MAT management for opioid addiction
  • Ongoing therapy and medication management
  • Prescription monitoring to prevent relapse

Frequently Asked Questions About Prescription Drug Treatment

What prescription drugs are commonly abused?

The most commonly abused prescription drugs include opioid painkillers (OxyContin, Vicodin, Percocet), benzodiazepines (Xanax, Valium, Klonopin), stimulants (Adderall, Ritalin), and sleep medications (Ambien, Lunesta). Each category requires specific treatment approaches for safe detox and recovery.

How does prescription drug addiction start?

Prescription drug addiction often begins with legitimate medical use. Over time, tolerance develops, requiring higher doses for the same effect. Physical dependence follows, causing withdrawal symptoms when stopping. Risk factors include longer duration of use, higher doses, personal or family history of addiction, and mental health conditions.

Is treatment different for prescription drugs vs. street drugs?

The medical treatment is similar—prescription opioids require the same MAT protocols as heroin. However, people addicted to prescription drugs often face unique psychological challenges like shame about misusing medications and difficulty accepting they have an addiction. Treatment addresses these specific needs.

What if I still need medication for my original condition?

Our physicians work with you to find non-addictive alternatives for managing pain, anxiety, ADHD, or sleep issues. Many effective treatments exist that don't carry addiction risk. We coordinate with your other providers to ensure continuity of care.

Will my doctor know I went to rehab?

Treatment records are protected by HIPAA and 42 CFR Part 2 (federal substance abuse confidentiality laws). We will not contact your prescribing doctors without your written consent. However, we encourage open communication with your healthcare providers for your safety.
