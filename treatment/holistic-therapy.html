What is Holistic Treatment?

Holistic treatment addresses the whole person—mind, body, and spirit—not just the symptoms of addiction. While evidence-based therapies like CBT and DBT form the foundation of our treatment, holistic therapies complement these approaches by promoting overall wellness and providing healthy ways to manage stress, process emotions, and find joy in recovery.

Years of substance use take a toll on every aspect of your being. Your body may be depleted and damaged. Your mind may be foggy and anxious. Your spirit may feel broken. Holistic therapies help restore balance and build the foundation for a healthy, sustainable recovery.

Benefits of Holistic Approaches

  • Stress Reduction: Natural ways to calm the nervous system and manage triggers
  • Physical Healing: Restore the body damaged by substance use
  • Emotional Processing: Non-verbal ways to work through feelings
  • Healthy Coping: Replace substance use with positive activities
  • Self-Discovery: Connect with interests and passions lost to addiction
  • Sustainable Recovery: Build lifestyle habits that support long-term sobriety

Our Holistic Therapies

Yoga

Yoga combines physical movement, breathing techniques, and mindfulness to reduce stress, improve body awareness, and build a healthy relationship with your body. Research shows yoga decreases anxiety, depression, and cravings while improving sleep and overall wellbeing. No experience necessary—our instructors adapt practices to all fitness levels.

Meditation & Mindfulness

Mindfulness meditation trains you to observe your thoughts and feelings without reacting automatically. This skill is invaluable in recovery—when cravings arise, you learn to notice them, understand they will pass, and choose not to act on them. We teach multiple meditation styles so you can find what works for you.

Art Therapy

Art therapy provides a way to express and process emotions that may be difficult to put into words. Led by licensed art therapists, these sessions use creative expression to explore feelings, reduce stress, and build self-awareness. No artistic talent required—the process matters, not the product.

Music Therapy

Music therapy uses listening, songwriting, and music-making to support emotional expression and healing. Music can access memories and emotions in unique ways, making it especially helpful for processing trauma and building emotional regulation skills.

Nutrition & Wellness

Substance use depletes your body of essential nutrients and disrupts healthy eating patterns. Our nutrition program helps restore physical health through balanced meals, nutritional education, and attention to gut health (which research links to mental health and addiction recovery).

Fitness & Movement

Regular exercise naturally boosts mood, reduces anxiety, improves sleep, and provides healthy stress relief. Our fitness programming includes gym access, outdoor activities, hiking, and movement classes adapted to all fitness levels.

Breathwork

Controlled breathing techniques can quickly calm the nervous system, reduce anxiety, and interrupt the stress response. Learn breathing exercises you can use anywhere to manage triggers, cravings, and difficult emotions.

Acupuncture

Acupuncture, particularly the NADA protocol developed specifically for addiction, can help reduce withdrawal symptoms, decrease cravings, improve sleep, and promote relaxation. Sessions are administered by licensed practitioners.

Integrating Holistic with Evidence-Based Care

Our holistic therapies don't replace evidence-based treatment—they enhance it. You'll receive proven therapies like CBT, DBT, and trauma-focused approaches alongside holistic practices. This integrated model gives you the best of both worlds: clinically proven interventions plus complementary therapies that support overall wellness and provide skills for lifelong recovery.

A Typical Day with Holistic Programming

  • Morning: Yoga or meditation to start the day centered
  • Mid-morning: Individual or group therapy (CBT, DBT, etc.)
  • Lunch: Nutritious meal in community
  • Afternoon: Psychoeducation groups, art/music therapy
  • Late afternoon: Fitness, outdoor activity, or free time
  • Evening: 12-step meeting, meditation, or process group

Building a Wellness-Based Lifestyle

The goal isn't just to get sober—it's to build a life you don't want to escape from. Holistic therapies help you discover (or rediscover) activities that bring genuine joy, peace, and fulfillment. When you leave treatment, you'll have:

  • Stress management techniques you can use daily
  • A regular exercise or movement practice
  • Understanding of how nutrition affects recovery
  • Creative outlets for emotional expression
  • Mindfulness skills for managing triggers
  • A foundation for ongoing self-care

Not "Soft" Treatment

Some people think holistic treatment means "easy" or "not serious." Nothing could be further from the truth. Our holistic programming is rigorous and challenging—you'll be asked to move out of your comfort zone, try new things, and commit to practices that may feel difficult at first. The work is hard, but the rewards are worth it.
