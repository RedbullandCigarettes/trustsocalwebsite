WARNING: Never Quit Benzos Cold Turkey

Benzodiazepine withdrawal can cause life-threatening seizures. Unlike opioids, stopping benzos abruptly can be fatal. Always detox under medical supervision with a proper tapering protocol.

Understanding Benzodiazepine Addiction

Benzodiazepines are prescription medications used to treat anxiety, insomnia, and seizures. According to the National Institute on Drug Abuse, benzos enhance the effect of GABA in the brain, producing sedation and relaxation. Physical dependence can develop in as little as 2-4 weeks of regular use.

Common Benzodiazepines We Treat

  • Alprazolam (Xanax): Short-acting, high addiction potential, severe withdrawal
  • Diazepam (Valium): Long-acting, used in tapering protocols
  • Clonazepam (Klonopin): Intermediate-acting, anxiety and seizure treatment
  • Lorazepam (Ativan): Intermediate-acting, often used for anxiety
  • Temazepam (Restoril): Used for insomnia

Signs of Benzodiazepine Addiction

  • Taking higher doses than prescribed
  • Running out of prescriptions early
  • Doctor shopping for multiple prescriptions
  • Inability to function without benzos
  • Experiencing withdrawal symptoms between doses
  • Mixing benzos with alcohol or opioids
  • Memory problems and cognitive impairment
  • Social withdrawal and neglected responsibilities
  • Continued use despite relationship or health problems

Counterfeit Pill Danger

Street "Xanax bars" are frequently counterfeit and may contain fentanyl. The DEA reports that 6 out of 10 fake pills contain a lethal dose of fentanyl. Only take benzodiazepines prescribed by a licensed physician.

Our Benzodiazepine Addiction Treatment Programs

Benzo detox requires specialized medical protocols. Our addiction medicine physicians create individualized tapering schedules to ensure safe, comfortable withdrawal.

Medical Detox with Tapering (2-8+ Weeks)

Safe benzo detox requires a gradual dose reduction. Tapering length depends on the specific benzo, dose, and duration of use:

  • Comprehensive medical evaluation and seizure risk assessment
  • Conversion to long-acting benzodiazepine (typically diazepam/Valium)
  • Gradual 10-25% dose reduction per week
  • 24/7 medical monitoring and seizure precautions
  • Comfort medications for anxiety, insomnia, and muscle tension
  • Adjustment of taper speed based on symptoms

Note: Benzo detox takes longer than most substances. Rushing the taper increases seizure risk and discomfort.

Why Tapering Is Essential

Benzodiazepines cause physical dependence through GABA receptor changes. Abrupt cessation causes:

  • Seizures: Can occur within 24-72 hours of stopping; potentially fatal
  • Delirium: Confusion, hallucinations, disorientation
  • Severe rebound anxiety: Worse than original anxiety condition
  • Psychosis: Paranoia and loss of touch with reality

Medical tapering prevents these dangerous complications while minimizing discomfort.

Residential Treatment (30-90 Days)

After completing or during the taper, comprehensive treatment addresses addiction:

  • Individual therapy for underlying anxiety disorders
  • Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) for anxiety management without medications
  • Dual diagnosis treatment for co-occurring conditions
  • Non-addictive anxiety management strategies
  • Stress reduction techniques (mindfulness, meditation, breathing exercises)
  • Education about the risks of returning to benzodiazepine use

Non-Addictive Alternatives for Anxiety

Part of benzo recovery involves learning to manage anxiety without sedatives:

  • SSRIs/SNRIs: Non-addictive antidepressants effective for anxiety
  • Buspirone: Non-addictive anti-anxiety medication
  • Cognitive Behavioral Therapy: Gold standard for anxiety treatment
  • Mindfulness and meditation: Proven anxiety reduction techniques
  • Exercise: Natural anxiety relief through endorphins
  • Hydroxyzine: Non-addictive as-needed anti-anxiety option

Benzodiazepine Withdrawal Timeline

Withdrawal severity and timeline vary based on the specific benzodiazepine, dose, and duration of use. This timeline represents unmedicated withdrawal—medical tapering significantly reduces these symptoms.

Hours 6-24: Early Withdrawal (Short-Acting Benzos)

Anxiety, restlessness, insomnia, increased heart rate, sweating, hand tremors

Days 1-4: Acute Withdrawal

Severe anxiety, panic attacks, insomnia, muscle tension, headaches, nausea, seizure risk highest

Days 5-14: Subacute Withdrawal

Continued anxiety, depression, irritability, difficulty concentrating, ongoing insomnia

Weeks to Months: Post-Acute Withdrawal (PAWS)

Intermittent anxiety, mood swings, sleep disturbances, cognitive difficulties, gradual improvement

With proper medical tapering, most acute withdrawal symptoms are avoided or significantly reduced.

Frequently Asked Questions About Benzo Treatment

Is benzodiazepine withdrawal dangerous?

Yes, benzodiazepine withdrawal can be life-threatening. Unlike opioid withdrawal, benzo withdrawal can cause seizures, which can be fatal. This is why medical detox with a proper tapering protocol is essential. Never attempt to quit benzodiazepines cold turkey—always seek medical supervision.

How long does benzo withdrawal last?

Benzo withdrawal duration depends on the specific drug and duration of use. Acute withdrawal from short-acting benzos (Xanax) may peak within 1-4 days. Long-acting benzos (Valium) may peak at 2-7 days. Post-acute withdrawal symptoms can last weeks to months. Medical tapering significantly reduces withdrawal severity.

What is benzo tapering?

Benzo tapering involves gradually reducing the benzodiazepine dose over time, often switching to a longer-acting benzo like diazepam (Valium) for smoother withdrawal. Tapering schedules vary from weeks to months depending on the individual's usage history. This approach minimizes withdrawal symptoms and prevents seizures.

Can I taper at home?

While some people with mild benzo dependence can taper at home under close physician supervision, it's generally safer to taper in a medical detox facility. This is especially true for high-dose users, those on short-acting benzos, or anyone with a history of seizures. Medical supervision provides safety and comfort.

Will I have anxiety forever after stopping benzos?

No. While rebound anxiety is common during withdrawal, it typically improves over time. The brain needs time to rebalance GABA systems. Non-addictive treatments like SSRIs, therapy, and lifestyle changes can effectively manage anxiety long-term without the risks of benzodiazepines.
