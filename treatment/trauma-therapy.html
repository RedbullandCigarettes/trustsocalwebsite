Understanding Trauma and Addiction

Trauma and addiction are deeply connected. Research shows that up to 75% of people seeking addiction treatment have experienced significant trauma. Many people first turn to drugs or alcohol as a way to cope with overwhelming pain, anxiety, or memories they can't escape.

At Trust SoCal, we understand that lasting recovery requires addressing not just the addiction, but the underlying trauma that drives it. Our trauma-informed approach creates safety, builds trust, and uses evidence-based therapies to help you heal.

Types of Trauma We Treat

  • Childhood Trauma: Abuse, neglect, household dysfunction, or adverse childhood experiences (ACEs)
  • Sexual Trauma: Sexual abuse, assault, or exploitation at any age
  • Physical Trauma: Accidents, injuries, medical trauma, or physical violence
  • Combat/Military Trauma: War experiences, military sexual trauma, moral injury
  • First Responder Trauma: Repeated exposure to death, violence, and emergencies
  • Domestic Violence: Intimate partner violence, coercive control, emotional abuse
  • Complex Trauma: Repeated or prolonged traumatic experiences over time

What is Trauma-Informed Care?

Trauma-informed care is an approach that recognizes the widespread impact of trauma and understands potential paths for recovery. Our trauma-informed principles include:

  • Safety: Creating physical and emotional safety throughout treatment
  • Trustworthiness: Building trust through transparency and consistency
  • Choice: Giving you control over your treatment decisions
  • Collaboration: Working together as partners in your recovery
  • Empowerment: Focusing on your strengths and resilience
  • Cultural Sensitivity: Respecting your background and identity

Our Trauma Therapies

EMDR Therapy

Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing helps your brain process traumatic memories without having to describe them in detail. Highly effective for PTSD and trauma-related addiction.

Somatic Experiencing

Body-based therapy that helps release trauma stored in your nervous system. Addresses physical symptoms of trauma like chronic tension, pain, and hypervigilance.

Trauma-Focused CBT

Cognitive behavioral therapy adapted specifically for trauma, helping you process traumatic events and develop healthy coping strategies.

Seeking Safety

An evidence-based program designed specifically for people with both trauma and addiction, focusing on building safety and coping skills.

Signs Trauma May Be Affecting You

  • Nightmares, flashbacks, or intrusive memories
  • Avoiding people, places, or things that remind you of trauma
  • Feeling numb, detached, or unable to feel positive emotions
  • Being easily startled or constantly on edge
  • Difficulty trusting others or maintaining relationships
  • Using substances to "turn off" your brain or escape memories
  • Feeling like you're "damaged" or different from others

You Don't Have to Share Everything

Many people avoid treatment because they're afraid of having to talk about their trauma. At Trust SoCal, we never pressure you to share more than you're comfortable with. Therapies like EMDR can help you heal without having to describe traumatic events in detail.
