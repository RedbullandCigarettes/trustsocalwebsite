Understanding Veteran Addiction

Veterans are at higher risk for addiction than the general population. The combination of combat trauma, pain from service-related injuries, difficulty transitioning to civilian life, and military culture that often normalized heavy drinking creates a perfect storm for substance use disorders.

At Trust SoCal, we understand that traditional treatment programs don't always work for veterans. Our veteran-focused programming respects military culture while addressing the unique challenges you faceâ€”including PTSD, moral injury, and the loss of military identity and brotherhood.

Why Veterans Struggle with Addiction

  • Combat Trauma/PTSD: Using substances to manage nightmares, hypervigilance, and intrusive memories
  • Chronic Pain: Service-related injuries often lead to prescription opioid dependence
  • Moral Injury: Guilt, shame, and spiritual wounds from actions in combat
  • Military Sexual Trauma (MST): Both men and women affected, often untreated
  • Loss of Identity: Struggling with who you are outside the military
  • Isolation: Missing the brotherhood/sisterhood of military service

Common Substances Among Veterans

  • Alcohol - Military culture often normalizes heavy drinking
  • Prescription opioids - Often started for legitimate service-related injuries
  • Benzodiazepines - Prescribed for anxiety, sleep, and PTSD symptoms
  • Heroin/Fentanyl - Often after prescription opioids become unavailable
  • Sleep medications - Self-medicating insomnia and nightmares
