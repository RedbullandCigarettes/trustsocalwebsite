Why Young Adults Need Specialized Treatment

They also have unique opportunities: the brain is still developing, making this an ideal time for lasting change.

Our young adult program brings together peers who understand each other's experiences. You won't be the youngest person in the room feeling out of placeâ€”you'll be surrounded by others your age who are also learning to build a life without substances.

Unique Challenges for Young Adults

  • Social Pressure: Substance use normalized among peers and in college culture
  • Identity Formation: Still figuring out who you are and what you want
  • Mental Health: High rates of anxiety, depression, ADHD, and trauma
  • Technology/Social Media: Comparison, FOMO, and isolation
  • Academic/Career Pressure: Stress about future success and expectations
  • Family Transition: Navigating independence while maintaining relationships

Our Young Adult Programming

Age-Specific Groups

Group therapy with peers your own age who understand the pressures you face. Discuss topics like relationships, social media, career anxiety, and building an identity separate from substances.

Life Skills Development

Many young adults in early addiction never learned essential life skills. We teach budgeting, cooking, time management, communication, and other practical skills for independent living.

Educational & Career Planning

Addiction may have derailed your education or career plans. We help you explore options, set goals, and develop a plan for returning to school or building a career in recovery.

Mental Health Treatment

Young adults have high rates of co-occurring anxiety, depression, ADHD, and trauma. Our dual diagnosis approach treats both addiction and mental health conditions.

Sober Social Skills

Learn to have fun, make friends, and date without substances. We help you build confidence in social situations and develop healthy relationship skills.

Common Substances Among Young Adults

  • Alcohol - Binge drinking and blackouts common in college culture
  • Marijuana - Often seen as "harmless" but can significantly impact brain development
  • Prescription stimulants - Adderall, Vyvanse for studying or weight loss
  • Benzodiazepines - Xanax for anxiety, often obtained from friends
  • Opioids - Including fentanyl-laced pills bought online or from dealers
  • MDMA/Ecstasy - Popular at festivals, clubs, and parties
  • Psychedelics - LSD, mushrooms, often used recreationally
