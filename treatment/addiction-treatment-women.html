Why Women Need Specialized Treatment

Women experience addiction differently than men. From biological factors to social pressures, the path into addictionâ€”and the path outâ€”has unique challenges that require specialized understanding and treatment approaches.

Research shows that women in gender-specific treatment programs have better outcomes, higher completion rates, and longer periods of sobriety compared to mixed-gender programs. At Trust SoCal, our women's programming creates a safe space to address issues that may be difficult to discuss in co-ed settings.

Unique Factors in Women's Addiction

  • Faster Progression: Women develop addiction faster than men ("telescoping effect")
  • Trauma Connection: 70%+ of women in treatment have trauma histories, often sexual
  • Co-occurring Disorders: Higher rates of depression, anxiety, and eating disorders
  • Relationship Patterns: Often introduced to substances by intimate partners
  • Childcare Responsibilities: Fear of losing children is both barrier and motivator
  • Stigma: Women face greater social stigma for addiction, delaying treatment

Our Women's Programming

Women-Only Groups

Process groups and therapy sessions exclusively for women, creating a safe environment to discuss trauma, relationships, body image, motherhood, and sexuality without fear of judgment or discomfort.

Trauma-Informed Care

Given the high prevalence of trauma among women with addiction, all our staff are trained in trauma-informed approaches. We offer EMDR therapy, trauma-focused groups, and Seeking Safety programming.

Co-occurring Mental Health

We address the depression, anxiety, and eating disorders that commonly accompany addiction in women. Our dual diagnosis program treats both conditions simultaneously.

Relationship & Codependency Work

Learn to recognize unhealthy relationship patterns, set boundaries, and build self-worth independent of others. We help you identify and break cycles of codependency.

Motherhood & Family

For mothers, we address the guilt and shame around how addiction has affected your children. Family therapy helps repair relationships and develop healthy parenting skills in recovery.

Common Substances Among Women

  • Alcohol - Most common substance of abuse among women
  • Prescription medications - Opioid painkillers, benzodiazepines, sleep aids
  • Opioids - Including heroin and fentanyl
  • Methamphetamine - Rising rates among women in recent years
  • Cocaine - Often used in social or professional settings

Support for Mothers

We understand that childcare responsibilities can be a major barrier to treatment. Trust SoCal can help you:

  • Navigate childcare arrangements during treatment
  • Maintain contact with your children when appropriate
  • Work with family court or CPS when necessary
  • Prepare for reunification and healthy parenting in recovery

What to Expect

In our women's program, you'll:

  • Participate in women-only therapy groups
  • Work with female therapists (when preferred)
  • Address trauma, relationships, and self-esteem
  • Learn stress management and self-care
  • Build a support network of women in recovery
  • Receive help with practical life issues
