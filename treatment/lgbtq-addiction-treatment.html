Why LGBTQ+ Affirming Treatment Matters

LGBTQ+ individuals face significantly higher rates of substance use disorders compared to the general population. Years of discrimination, family rejection, minority stress, and the trauma of living in a world that hasn't always been accepting take a tollâ€”and many LGBTQ+ people use substances to cope.

At Trust SoCal, we provide genuinely affirming care. This isn't just "tolerance"â€”it's celebration of who you are. Our staff are trained in LGBTQ+ cultural competency, and you'll never have to hide or minimize your identity to fit in. Your authentic self is welcome here.

Factors Contributing to Higher Addiction Rates

  • Minority Stress: Chronic stress from discrimination, prejudice, and identity concealment
  • Family Rejection: Many LGBTQ+ people faced rejection from family of origin
  • Trauma: Higher rates of bullying, harassment, violence, and sexual assault
  • Internalized Shame: Growing up in environments that condemned your identity
  • Social Settings: Bars and clubs historically central to LGBTQ+ community
  • Healthcare Barriers: Fear of discrimination prevents many from seeking help

Our LGBTQ+ Affirming Approach

Affirming Environment

From intake to aftercare, you'll be treated with respect and dignity. We use correct names and pronouns, and all staff receive ongoing LGBTQ+ cultural competency training. You'll never be asked to change who you are.

LGBTQ+ Specific Groups

Optional LGBTQ+ process groups where you can discuss identity-related issues, coming out, relationships, and community connection with peers who understand your experiences.

Trauma-Informed Care

Many LGBTQ+ individuals carry trauma from discrimination, rejection, or violence. Our trauma therapies help you heal these wounds without being retraumatized.

Identity & Self-Acceptance

Work on internalized homophobia, biphobia, or transphobia that may contribute to shame and substance use. Build a positive relationship with your identity.

Chosen Family & Community

Connect with LGBTQ+ affirming support networks, 12-step meetings, and sober social spaces. Learn to build community without substances.

Issues We Address

  • Coming out and identity development at any age
  • Family rejection and chosen family
  • Discrimination and minority stress
  • LGBTQ+-specific relationship issues
  • Body image and eating concerns
  • HIV/AIDS and associated trauma
  • Chemsex and party-and-play (PnP) patterns
  • Internalized shame and self-acceptance
  • Building sober LGBTQ+ community

For Transgender & Non-Binary Clients

We affirm and support transgender and non-binary individuals throughout treatment:

  • Correct name and pronoun use by all staff and in documentation
  • Continuation of hormone therapy during treatment
  • Accommodations respecting gender identity
  • Support for gender-related trauma and discrimination
  • Connection to trans-affirming providers for ongoing care

You're Not Alone

LGBTQ+ people in recovery build some of the strongest, most supportive communities. In treatment and beyond, you'll find people who understand you, accept you, and will walk alongside you in recovery. You don't have to do this alone.
